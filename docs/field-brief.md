# OPSBOARD Operational Briefing

### Executive Summary: What is OPSBOARD?
OPSBOARD is a simple tool designed to help the WUF13 Guest Services team stay organized during the event. It acts as a central digital logbook where you can quickly record "Incidents" (problems that need solving) and "Operations" (routine daily tasks). Its main goal is to make sure everyone sees the same information in real-time, even when the internet is slow or unreliable, so the team can coordinate better than using just WhatsApp or radio.

### Your Role: Key Differences
As a **Coordinator**, you are the primary person using the app in the field. Here is how your role differs from others:

*   **Coordinator:** You create new incidents, write progress notes, and log routine updates. You can save "drafts" if the internet cuts out.
*   **Manager:** They have the authority to "Escalate" an incident (marking it as high priority) or "Resolve" it (marking it as finished). They also open and close the work shifts.
*   **Admin:** They handle the behind-the-scenes setup, such as adding new staff accounts or changing the names of zones and services.

### Happy Path: How to Use OPSBOARD
To ensure your data is recorded correctly, follow these steps:
1.  **Login:** Enter your email and password.
2.  **Check Shift Status:** Look at the top bar. If it says "NO ACTIVE SHIFT," wait for a Manager to start the shift before you begin.
3.  **Capture Information:** Use the "New Incident" button for problems or "Log Operation" for routine tasks.
4.  **Submit:** Always press "Submit." Once submitted, your entry becomes a permanent part of the event record.

### Working Offline
If the Wi-Fi fails or you lose your connection, the app will show an **OFFLINE** indicator in the top bar. You can still type and save new incidents or notes. These are saved as **Drafts** on your tablet. When your internet returns, the app will try to send these drafts automatically. You must visit the **DRAFTS** screen to make sure your work was sent successfully.

### When to Escalate vs. When to Try Again
*   **Escalate:** If an incident is serious and needs a Manager's immediate attention, tell your Manager. They will move it to the "NOW" screen so everyone can see it is a priority.
*   **Try Again:** If you see a "REJECTED" or "NEEDS REVIEW" message on a draft, it means the information didn't reach the server. Go to the Drafts screen and try to submit it again once you have a better signal.

### Important Quotes & Insights
> "The system becomes more reliable than fragmented WhatsApp or chat-based coordination." 

**Context:** OPSBOARD is meant to be the single "source of truth." While chat is good for talking, OPSBOARD is where the official record lives.

**Actionable Insight:** The "NOW" screen does not always show new incidents the second they are created. To see the most recent updates from other team members, **refresh the page** manually every few minutes.

### Password Changes
You should change your password the first time you log in. Look for the **Key (🔑) button** in the top bar of the app. This will open a window where you can set a new, private password.

### Call Tech Support Criteria
**Call for technical support only if: The page is down (you see a blank screen or a "502" error) and does not come back after three refreshes.**

**When in doubt, ask a Manager.**
